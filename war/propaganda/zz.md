
# War Propaganda Citations:

"School based propaganda dates back to 1917 when President Wilson commissioned the nation’s first governmental propaganda agency, the Committee on Public Information (CPI). Schools were targeted because they were a way to reach a large population and carefully crafted messages could be disseminated to both educators and students thus engineering consensus on key governmental policies related to the war. Wilson’s strategy was highly successful in gaining support for the war and established a precedent for the use of educational propaganda during military conflicts."  
[https://pdxscholar.library.pdx.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1104&context=younghistorians](https://pdxscholar.library.pdx.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1104&context=younghistorians)
